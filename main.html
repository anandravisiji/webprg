Tribute to A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Dr. Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India. He was an Indian aerospace scientist and politician who played a crucial role in India's space and missile development programs. Kalam graduated in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, and later studied aerospace engineering at the Madras Institute of Technology (MIT).

Kalam joined the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO) and later the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). He was a key figure in the development of the Indian satellite launch vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite in near-earth orbit in 1980, making India an exclusive member of the Space Club.

He became known as the Missile Man of India for his work on the development of ballistic missile and launch vehicle technology. Kalam played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, solidifying India's status as a nuclear weapon state.

In 2002, Kalam was elected as the 11th President of India. During his presidency, he was affectionately known as the People's President for his accessibility and humility. After completing his term, he continued to contribute to education, writing, and public service.

Dr. Kalam was a recipient of several prestigious awards, including the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor. He passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Shillong, leaving behind a legacy of inspiration and dedication to the nation.
